About the Poet ุดุงุนุฑ ฺฉุง ุชุนุงุฑู

Moeed ul Hassan explores the intersection of human psychology and poetic expression, drawing inspiration from behavioral science, particularly the principles found in works like "Atomic Habits" by James Clear. His poetry delves into the mechanics of human nature, habit formation, and the subtle ways our environment shapes our identity.

Writing in both English and Urdu, Moeed bridges cultures and languages to explore universal themes of growth, connection, mortality, and the quest for meaning. His work reflects a deep understanding of how tiny changes compound into profound transformations, both in life and in verse.

Through his bilingual poetry, he seeks to make the abstract concepts of psychology and self-improvement accessible through the emotional power of verse, believing that poetry can serve as both mirror and compass for the human experience.
